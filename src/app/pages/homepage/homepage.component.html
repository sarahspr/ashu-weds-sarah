I was picking my friend Surabhi from campus to hang out when she asked if I could also give a ride home to her friend and his roommate. I said "sure" and let everyone cram into my falling apart 2002 blue Volkswagen Beetle. Surabhi and her friend got into the back seat while her friend's roommate, soon to be known as Ashu, squeezed into the front seat next to me. The first thing that I noticed about him was his appearance. He was pretty tall and really cute.

I introduced myself and asked him what his name was and where he was from in India. Of course at that point I had to tell him I had studied abroad in India and speak Hindi, looking for the "oh wowww" response I am usually met with. Ashu's response though? "Oh okay." I think he was the first person from India I have ever spoken to that didn't think it was cool that I'd lived there and spoke their native language so I was a little confused as to how to carry the conversation foward. I asked him a few basic questions about himself while he responded quietly with half sentences. "Either this guy already doesn't like me for some reason or he's really shy" I thought.

A few weeks later Surabhi told me about an incident regarding a bicycle theft. Her friend had won a bicycle in an on-campus raffle and the roommates had been sharing the bike commuting to South Campus before taking the bus to North Campus. Nobody was supposed to leave the bike on South Campus; they had to place the bicycle on the bike rack on the front of the campus bus and take it to North Campus with them so the bike wouldn't get stolen. (I don't know about you but I've never done that and I sure would feel like an idiot trying to figure out how that works). Apparently Ashu took the bike with him that day and left it on South Campus. Naturally, the bike was stolen. "What a jerk", I thought for not taking the bike to North Campus with him.
